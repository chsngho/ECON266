Does the sample mean seem to converge faster, slower, or independently of the variance?

The sample mean seems to converge faster when it has a lower variance.

To explain this in more detail, the consequence of plotting different values for the standard deviation is clearly shown by the graph. When I changed the value to 2, instead of 1, not only did the range of the y-axis (running_mean) expand, but also, unlike the original graph, where the fluctuation of the line becomes steadied as it gets closer to the end, this new graph showed a nonstop fluctuation. On the other hand, in the case where I put 0.2 instead of 1, although the initial starting point is still fluctuating, shortly after the first part, I was able to see that the variance of the entire graph is a lot steadier than the two graphs that I have created previously.
